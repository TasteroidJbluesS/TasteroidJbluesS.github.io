Professional Self-Assessment

Completing the Computer Science program and developing this ePortfolio has provided an opportunity to reflect on both my technical growth and my professional development. Throughout the program, I developed experience in software engineering, algorithms and data structures, databases, full-stack development, artificial intelligence, and secure computing practices. As my knowledge expanded, I became increasingly interested in cybersecurity and the role security plays in every aspect of modern software systems.

Successful technology projects require more than technical expertise alone. Developers must communicate effectively with stakeholders, understand project requirements, explain technical concepts to non-technical audiences, and work collaboratively toward shared goals. My coursework and customer-facing professional experience helped me develop communication skills that complement my technical background.

The CS 360 Mobile Application artifact demonstrates software engineering principles including modularity, maintainability, validation, usability, defensive programming, and software architecture. The CS 370 Pirate Intelligent Agent project demonstrates algorithmic thinking through reinforcement learning, adaptive epsilon decay, reward shaping, and state-memory tracking. The CS 465 Travlr Getaways application demonstrates database design and full-stack development through MongoDB schema validation, indexing, query optimization, and API integration.

Security has become one of the most important themes throughout my academic journey and is the area of computer science that most closely aligns with my future career goals. Concepts related to secure coding, authentication, validation, access control, defensive programming, and data protection appeared repeatedly throughout the curriculum. These experiences helped me develop a stronger security mindset and further solidified my interest in pursuing a career in cybersecurity.
